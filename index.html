Nicolas Winton

A man who save 100's of people from death.

Here's a time line of Nicholas Winton

  • Born May 19, 1909 Hampstead, London, England
  • Entered Stowe School in 1923
  • 1931 moved to France to work from Banque Nationale de Credit in Paris
  • Selected for British fencing team in 1938 in hopes of competing in Olympics which were cancelled due to the war
  • November 1938 Neitherlands closed borders to Nazi occupied Germany
  • Winton successfully took 669 Jewish children on a train through the Neitherlands to Britain
  • Summer of 1939 placed photographs of children seeking families to house children
  • Last group of children, 250 total, were set to leave Prague on September 1, 1939 but Hilter's invasion of Poland started 2nd World War
  • 1940 joined the Royal Air Force as aircraftman
  • On August 17, 1944 was promoted to pilot officer on probabtionary basis
  • February 17, 1945 was officially promoted flying officer
  • Winton relinquished his commission on May 19, 1954
  • Post war and military duty Winton began work at International Refugee Organization
  • Met Grete Gjelstrup, a Danish secretary and were married on October 31, 1948
  • The couple had three children: Nick, Barbara, and Robin
  • It wasn't until 1988 his wife found a scrapbook in the attic detailing the children he rescued
  • In 2003 received the Pride of Britain Award for Lifetime Achievement
  • Winton was name British Hero of the Holocaust by British Government in 2010
  • Died July 1, 2015 at age of 106
